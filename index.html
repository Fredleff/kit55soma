THE PROBLEM

Every team you hired is hitting its targets. Every contract is being met. On paper, everyone is winning.

And the project is dying anyway.

This is not bad luck. It is not bad people. The parts are too good.

When the old vertical broke apart, the pieces became independent businesses. They got sharper, leaner, more expert. But each one now runs multiple projects. Their economics and attention are split by design.

No one owns the space between the experts. That is exactly where the project bleeds. It starts as a drop. It compounds in the gaps until it becomes a river, and the river always runs to the same floor: the owner, the investors, the principal carrying the real risk.

We are not here to make the experts better. They are already better.

We are here to make them congruent.
